GEORGETOWN DAY SCHOOL'S PREMIER STUDENT NEWSPAPER

October 24, 2022

Welcome back to the Bit Newsletter! Today, we’re covering the beginning of Yom Fox’s tenure as principal and her interactions with students; community members’ impressions of a GDS bias incident reporting tool; and a history teacher who was among the finalists for a national award. We also have a collection of photos from Country Market Day. Opinions has pieces on Highlighter Elimination’s significance and a writer’s experiences with airport customs officials, plus a letter to the editor from a distressed jazz teacher. Sports has stories on the freshman volleyball team and a recent men’s varsity soccer victory. And The Augur Bite has a recipe for a Chinese dessert. Don’t hesitate to reach out with any questions, suggestions or letters to the editor.

We also have some exciting news to share about the Bit: Nava Mach, a Bit illustrator, was named one of ten finalists nationwide for the National Scholastic Press Association’s 2021-2022 Artist of the Year Award for a portfolio of her digital drawings. Check out a selection of her work from last school year compiled here. And in the comic strip category, “Chadwick the Cheerio Applies to College” by Julius Boxer-Cooper ’22 was a finalist.

In First Months, Fox Observes High School, After Iffy Start With Seniors

By Nico David-Fox

High School Principal Yom Fox has spent the early part of the school year getting acquainted with GDS, fitting visits to classes and breakfasts with seniors into a schedule packed with administrative meetings. Fox’s relationship with the student body began with uncertainty, as concerns about her intentions to change school customs spiraled into misconstrued rumors on the senior trip. But Fox told the Bit she is observing the community and asking questions to better understand it before seeking to implement new policies. This story examines the start of her tenure and students’ varied levels of interaction with their new principal.

Students See Bias Reporting Tool’s Potential But Have Concerns

By Ila Dohrmann

GDS’ diversity, equity and inclusion office introduced in February and again in class meetings last month a tool students can use to report instances of bias or discrimination they encounter at school. Students who spoke to the Bit generally supported the idea, but some expressed hesitations about how well it will work. This article explains the tool—a Google form—and community members’ impressions of it.

Lisa Rauschart, Admired at GDS for Her Dedication, Wins National Recognition

By Catherine Dooley

History teacher Lisa Rauschart was recognized last month as one of the ten finalists nationwide for a teaching award given by the Gilder Lehrman Institute of American History, which had already named her its 2022 D.C. History Teacher of the Year. Students and colleagues interviewed by the Bit praised Rauschart for the effort she brings to her classes. Read this story to learn more about Rauschart’s career and the award process.

Photo

In Photos: Country Market Day’s Davenport Street Debut

By Olivia Brown and Kaiden J. Yu

Country Market Day, GDS’ customary fall fair, happened on Saturday for the first time since the pandemic began and for the first time on the unified campus. See Bit photographers’ shots of some of the day’s numerous events.

Opinions

Highlighter Elimination Is Quintessentially GDS

By Luca Pedroni

The game of Highlighter Elimination is entering its third week. This piece argues that the widespread excitement about the game and the interactions it produces—and even its renaming—embody the GDS community’s special spirit.

I Cherish My Identity, No Matter How Many Customs Officials Undermine It

By Nick von Hindenburg

In a personal essay, a Bit Opinions writer reflects on his pride in his multiracial identity, his experiences living on three continents and his frustrating encounters with officials at airports who question his relation to his mother.

Letter to the Editor: ‘Jazzlike, Jazz-Influenced, Even Jazz-Adjacent’—Just Not ‘Jazzy’

An Augur Beat column about music at Art All Night called one GDS band’s performance “jazzy.” Jazz teacher Brad Linde wrote to the editors of the Bit to denounce that word choice.

Sports

New All-Freshman Volleyball Team Sparks Excitement Among Young Players

By Sloane Holder

The volleyball program added a freshman team this fall after its competitive success in 2021 gave rise to increased enthusiasm for the sport at GDS. This story offers a look at the program’s growth.

After Recent Win, Men’s Soccer Hopes to Strengthen Skills for Last Games

By Sabine Hletko Wood

GDS’ men’s varsity soccer team beat Sidwell Friends last Tuesday and is now looking to the MAC tournament that will begin this week. This article recaps that Oct. 18 game and the team’s outlook on the rest of its season.

GDS Sports Bulletin—Week of Oct. 17

The weekly score bulletin has fall teams’ results from last week and a listing of next week’s competitions, which include league tournament games or championships for women’s varsity tennis, men’s varsity soccer and cross country.
